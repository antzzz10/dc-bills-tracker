Urgent — House Vote Expected Tuesday, February 3

Congress Is Trying to Take DC's Money

A disapproval resolution (H.J.Res. 142) would overturn DC's local tax law, drain hundreds of millions in revenue, and kill tax credits reaching 78,000 children.

What's happening

In November, the DC Council passed temporary legislation to unlink the District's tax code from costly federal tax cuts in the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). That law prevents DC from losing $658 million in local revenue over five years.

Congress is now fast-tracking a disapproval resolution to overturn that local decision. The House is expected to debate and vote on H.J.Res. 142 on Tuesday. The Senate committee markup on the companion resolution (S.J.Res. 102) is scheduled for the same week.

Ten other states have taken similar decoupling action. Only DC faces a congressional override.

What's at stake

The OBBBA tax cuts overwhelmingly benefit DC households with incomes over $1.3 million. Those households still get their federal tax cut. DC simply chose not to replicate those cuts locally.

Instead, the Council invested saved revenue in two proven anti-poverty tools:

  • Expanded Earned Income Tax Credit — accelerated to a full 100% local match of the federal EITC
  • New $1,000-per-child tax credit — for families earning under $75K (individuals) / $90K (couples)

Together, these credits are expected to reach 78,000 children and reduce child poverty in DC by 20%.

If the disapproval resolution passes, all of this goes away — and DC risks another credit downgrade. Moody's already downgraded DC's rating after a 2025 continuing resolution forced $1 billion in local spending cuts.

Why this matters

DC residents had no vote on the federal tax changes Congress passed. The Council acted within its authority to protect local revenue and direct it toward working families. This resolution would override that decision — stripping tax relief from the residents who need it most, to preserve tax cuts for those who need them least.
